**SECTION 2**

**The Problem Krovos Solves**

Most of the things that shape our lives are never taught. School covers geometry but not how to file taxes. Trigonometry but not how to evaluate a job offer. The periodic table but not how to choose insurance. People are expected to navigate their most consequential decisions — buying a home, preparing for a child, planning for retirement, caring for aging parents — without adequate preparation.

| **The Knowledge Gap** People genuinely don't know — not because they lack intelligence, but because they were never taught. Krovos builds education into every guidance moment. | **The Clarity Gap** Too much information, not enough signal. The internet created abundance without understanding. Krovos translates information into understanding for this specific person. | **The Activation Gap** People know what to do and still don't. Not laziness — paralysis. Krovos reduces the distance between knowing and doing through specific next steps and friction reduction. |

**SECTION 3**

**Product Architecture**

**The Life Graph**

The Life Graph is the core data structure — a persistent, structured understanding of a person's complete life situation. It is populated through document extraction and guided intake, and deepens over time as life events update it.

Key sections: identity (name, age, family structure), income (by person, all sources), housing (mortgage, equity), investments (by account type and owner), debt, tax, insurance, retirement goals, spending, and life phase context.

**Smart Start — 10-Step Onboarding**

Document-first onboarding that reads up to 26+ document types via AI extraction. The burden of being understood belongs to Krovos, not the user. 10 steps: Identity → Documents → Confirm → Housing → Income → Spending → Investments → Debt → Retirement Goals → Review.

**Life Understanding Report**

The recognition moment. Six sections: Your Situation (recognition) → Financial Snapshot → What You Are Carrying (burden inventory) → Your Opportunities → What To Do First (priority guidance) → Moving Forward. Every section grounded in this person's actual data.

**Navigation Engines — By Life Phase**

Phase 1 (Financial Foundation): emergency fund, debt optimizer, paycheck allocation, retirement trajectory. Phase 2 (Household): family planning, parental leave, children's education. Phase 3 (Optimizer): open enrollment comparison, tax planning, career navigation. Phase 4 (Young Adult): college decisions, early career. Phase 5 (Modular Marketplace): estate planning, home buying, caregiver support.

**The Trusted Guide Layer**

Persistent AI with full Life Graph context. Demonstrates understanding actively — never makes the user re-explain. Orients to life phase and active decisions before responding. Ends every exchange with a specific, actionable next step.

**SECTION 4**

**Life Phases — The Organizing Principle**

Krovos organizes around life phases, not demographics. Where a person is in their life determines what they actually need — not how old they are, how much they earn, or what financial products they own.

Life phases are inferred from the Life Graph — never self-reported, never shown as a label. A 28-year-old expecting their first child is in a different life phase than another 28-year-old who is single. Krovos meets people where they are.

| **Foundation** | Building the base — first jobs, first apartments, student loan management, starting retirement contributions, emergency fund construction |
| --- | --- |
| **Building** | Growing complexity — home ownership, dual incomes, early family formation, career advancement, first major financial decisions |
| **Expansion** | Maximum complexity — children in household, mortgage, multiple competing financial priorities, career peak |
| **Optimization** | Efficiency phase — kids growing, debt reducing, wealth building accelerating, retirement planning intensifying |
| **Pre-Retirement** | Final approach — portfolio protection, Social Security strategy, pension optimization, healthcare bridge planning |
| **Transition** | Major change — divorce, career change, job loss, inheritance, caring for aging parents |
| **Starting Over** | Rebuilding — after divorce, after loss, after setback, returning to work, rebuilding financial foundation |
