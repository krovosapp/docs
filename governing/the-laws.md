**KROVOS**

The Laws

 

Ten governing principles.

They apply in every product decision, every AI interaction,

every partnership conversation, and every line of copy.

***Not guidelines. Not suggestions. Laws.***

v1.0  |  2026

**Before the Laws**

Every institution needs rules. Rules without conviction are just compliance theater — followed when convenient, ignored under pressure. Krovos's Laws are not rules in that sense. They are the operational expression of the conviction underneath the company.

The Constitution governs character. The Foundation Stones govern belief. The Laws govern behavior — the specific, testable commitments that determine whether Krovos is actually being Krovos in every interaction, every day.

There are ten. They are ordered. The first two are the most fundamental — everything else cascades from them. Laws 3 through 7 govern how the relationship is built and held. Laws 8 through 10 govern how the company measures and governs itself.

No law overrides another. All ten apply simultaneously. When a decision appears to create tension between them, that tension is a signal to examine the decision more carefully — not to choose which law to suspend.

***A company that keeps these Laws keeps its character. A company that makes exceptions loses it gradually, then suddenly.***

**The Ten Laws**

| **1.  ****Understand before guiding — always.** This is the law beneath all other laws. Krovos never begins with recommendations. It begins with understanding. No product feature, no AI response, no piece of copy should ever guide without first establishing that the person's situation has been understood. If there is not yet enough understanding to guide well, the correct action is to seek more understanding — not to guess. **Violation looks like:  ***A recommendation that could have been made without knowing anything specific about this person.* |
| --- |

| **2.  ****Recognition before recommendation — always.** Before Krovos tells someone what to do, it tells them what it sees. Recognition — the moment a person feels genuinely understood — is the foundation of trust. Trust is the foundation of every recommendation landing. A recommendation made before recognition has been established is a recommendation made to a stranger. Krovos does not guide strangers. It guides people it has understood. **Violation looks like:  ***Jumping to action items before reflecting back what was heard. A report that opens with priorities before it opens with understanding.* |
| --- |

| **3.  ****Minimize the cost of being understood.** Being understood should feel effortless. Every question Krovos asks, every form it requires, every piece of information it requests — all of it should be the minimum necessary to understand well. If something can be inferred, it should not be asked. If something can be uploaded once, it should not be typed repeatedly. The burden of being understood belongs to Krovos, not to the person. **Violation looks like:  ***A long onboarding form that asks for things Krovos could have inferred from a document. Re-asking for information the person already provided.* |
| --- |

| **4.  ****Reduce the burden of navigating life.** Every interaction with Krovos should leave a person carrying less than they were carrying before. Not more. Not the same. Less. Features that add complexity without resolving it violate this law. Recommendations that open new questions without closing existing ones violate this law. If someone finishes an interaction with Krovos feeling more overwhelmed than when they started, something went wrong. **Violation looks like:  ***A feature that surfaces ten new things to worry about without resolving any existing ones. A report that identifies burdens without prioritizing or pathing any of them.* |
| --- |

| **5.  ****Never profit from confusion.** Clarity must always increase. If Krovos's business model, product design, or AI behavior ever depends on a person not fully understanding what is happening — that model, design, or behavior must be changed. This law applies to pricing, to partner recommendations, to notification design, to the framing of options. Confusion is never a feature. It is a failure. **Violation looks like:  ***A pricing page that obscures what is included. A partner recommendation that does not disclose the referral relationship. A notification designed to generate urgency without surfacing the real stakes.* |
| --- |

| **6.  ****Tell the truth with compassion.** Honesty is not optional. Krovos will tell people what they need to hear — even when it is uncomfortable, even when it contradicts what they want to believe, even when the truth creates short-term friction. But honesty is delivered with the full picture: what is true, what it means, what the options are. Truth without compassion is alarm. Compassion without truth is flattery. Neither serves the person. **Violation looks like:  ***Softening a difficult truth to the point of inaccuracy. Delivering bad news without a path forward. Staying silent about something important because raising it might cause friction.* |
| --- |

| **7.  ****The relationship is the product — protect it above all else.** The technology changes. The features change. The product roadmap changes. What must not change is the quality and integrity of the relationship Krovos builds with the people who trust it. Every decision that would compromise that relationship for short-term gain — a revenue opportunity, a growth shortcut, a partnership that trades on user trust — violates this law. The relationship is not a means to an end. It is the end. **Violation looks like:  ***A business model decision that prioritizes conversion over trust. A product feature that increases engagement at the expense of user wellbeing. A partnership that monetizes user attention rather than serving user interest.* |
| --- |

| **8.  ****Life phases, not demographics.** Krovos organizes around where a person is in their life, not who they are as a demographic. Age, income, gender, location — these are not the organizing variables. Life phase is. A recommendation system organized around demographics produces generic guidance. A recommendation system organized around life phase produces relevant guidance. This law governs product design, AI behavior, content strategy, and marketing. **Violation looks like:  ***Segmenting users by age bracket instead of life situation. A recommendation that would be correct for a 30-year-old but not for this specific 30-year-old in their specific life phase. Marketing that targets **"**millennials**"** instead of **"**people navigating their first home purchase.**"* |
| --- |

| **9.  ****Peace of mind is the metric.** Not usage. Not DAU. Not session length. Not feature adoption. Peace of mind. The question that governs every product and business decision is: does this move people toward or away from peace of mind? A feature that drives engagement but increases anxiety fails this test. A capability that is rarely used but closes a significant open loop passes it. Success is measured by whether people are better equipped to navigate their lives — not by whether they open the app more often. **Violation looks like:  ***Optimizing a feature for re-engagement without asking whether re-engagement serves the user. Measuring success by session length rather than burden reduction.* |
| --- |

| **10.  ****The user****'****s interest governs every decision.** This is the law that sits over all others when they appear to conflict. When the business interest and the user interest diverge, the user wins. Not because the business is unimportant — it is essential — but because the business only exists as long as the trust exists. Every decision that trades user interest for business convenience trades the long-term for the short-term. Krovos does not make that trade. **Violation looks like:  ***A recommendation made because it generates partner revenue rather than because it is the best option for the user. Any situation where **"**what is good for Krovos**"** and **"**what is good for the user**"** are treated as competing considerations rather than aligned ones.* |
| --- |
