## Scoring Method

Each guide is scored out of 100:

| Dimension | Weight | What earns full credit |
|---|---:|---|
| Decision utility | 20 | Helps the user make consequential decisions, not merely understand a topic |
| Completeness | 15 | Covers the realistic life-event scope and the expensive-to-miss questions |
| Actionability | 15 | Provides a sequenced, usable plan with deadlines and next actions |
| Personalized tools | 20 | Topic-specific tools use the user's real data and produce useful saved outputs |
| Differentiation | 15 | Offers a defensible advantage over strong free resources and isolated calculators |
| Safety and currency | 10 | High-stakes claims are current, qualified, sourced, and routed to professional help appropriately |
| Core-product integration | 5 | Clearly improves the Life Graph, Krovos Guide, or future recommendations |

Grade bands: A = 90–100; B+ = 87–89; B = 83–86; B- = 80–82; C+ = 77–79; C = 73–76; C- = 70–72; D = below 70.

A high score does not override a safety or factual blocker. A guide with a material legal, tax, benefits, or product-access error remains on hold until corrected.

## Guide-by-Guide Upgrade Requirements

### Early Career

**What works:** The guide correctly connects first-paycheck behavior, employer benefits, debt, emergency savings, housing, and mixed W-2/1099 income. First Job Decisions, Student Loan Repayment Planner, and First Apartment Calculator are meaningfully phase-specific.

**What prevents overdelivery:** The action list is generic and does not become the user's plan. There is no single result showing how the first offer, benefits elections, debt minimums, apartment move, and emergency-fund target fit in the same monthly cash flow.

**Upgrade completed July 20, 2026:** The Guide now produces a saved 90-day launch plan, benefits-election summary, employer-match record, first-paycheck allocation, official student-loan decision record, and apartment-readiness outcome using existing Life Graph context without another report. First Job Decisions no longer treats take-home pay as gross compensation or tells members Roth “wins.” Student Loan Repayment Planner no longer models the invalid SAVE plan, fabricates IDR/forgiveness estimates, or labels a path the Krovos recommendation; it saves results from current official tools and confirms loan timing and PSLF status. First Apartment Calculator now uses written move-in amounts, protects entered post-move emergency cash, removes universal rent-ratio verdicts, and saves the decision. Official DOL, IRS, Education, FSA, and credit-report sources plus review triggers are embedded.

### Newlywed

**What works:** It treats the wedding and the marriage as one financial lifecycle rather than unrelated topics. The combined/separate/hybrid model, wedding budget, filing status, beneficiaries, insurance, and money-date structure create a coherent bundle.

**What prevents overdelivery:** State property rules, tax elections, name/account administration, and financial-safety issues are not sufficiently operationalized.

**Upgrade completed July 20, 2026:** The Guide now produces a complete wedding funding path, a couple-selected account model and contribution rule, a first-year tax confirmation record, and a post-marriage coverage, beneficiary, ownership, name-change, estate, and tax handoff. It includes official IRS, HealthCare.gov, SSA, and DOL source layers; routes state-law and complex-family issues to the appropriate professional; and provides a private path for users experiencing financial control or abuse. Joint Finances Comparison no longer invents different investment outcomes for bookkeeping models, Wedding Budget Planner protects an emergency-fund floor and includes rings in the complete cost, and Filing Status Comparison uses corrected 2026 MFJ/MFS rules while clearly remaining a limited screen.

### Immigration

**What works:** Work-authorization gaps, U.S. credit building, immigration-process costs, cross-border retirement, and remittances are a distinctive combination. The Work Authorization Gap Planner can be a genuine premium anchor.

**Blocking issues:** During this audit, the Markdown source was corrected where it said a passport was accepted “universally,” generalized IRA eligibility by immigration/tax-residency label rather than taxable compensation, and used the wrong 2026 gift exclusion. Those corrections are not live until published to Supabase. Broad housing and public-benefit statements still require a complete expert review. The guide also has no standardized action plan, Krovos Guide prompt set, or official source list.

**Required upgrade:** Rebuild around a user-selected status and decision rather than a single broad narrative. Link current USCIS fees and processing times, IRS international-tax rules, official banking guidance, and qualified legal/tax referrals. Never infer legal authorization from financial inputs.

### Home Buying

**What works:** The guide is strongest where it rejects lender preapproval as a household budget, compares rent and buy across time horizons, and includes the cash costs after closing. The tool set covers down payment/PMI, total ownership cost, closing, and escrow.

**What prevents overdelivery:** Rules of thumb are presented more prominently than user-specific constraints. PMI, inspection contingencies, assistance programs, taxes, insurance, and closing practices vary by loan and location.

**Required upgrade:** Save a property comparison with total cash-to-close, post-close reserves, monthly ownership cost, break-even horizons, and risk flags. Add a milestone checklist from preapproval through the first year of ownership and current official sources.

**July 20 upgrade progress:** The source was rebuilt around a five-minute personalized path and an explicit Krovos-prepares/user-confirms/saved-outcome contract. Generic housing ratios are no longer presented as the member's budget. The Guide now produces a durable property decision record, short/expected/long holding-period comparison, cash-to-close and post-close plan, financing and property milestones, assistance-term review, fair-housing-safe location research, professional-handoff triggers, and a dated official-source layer. It explicitly works without another AI report. Rent vs. Buy, Homeownership Cost, and Closing Cost Calculator now save their material assumptions and outcomes to the Home Buying plan. The closing tool no longer asserts a static list of states where an attorney is required, and PMI language now distinguishes an estimated 80% request point from automatic termination and program-specific rules.

**Still required before release:** Apply and verify the guide migrations; publish the revised Markdown to Supabase; add Life Graph prefill where property inputs can be safely reused; persist the Down Payment vs. PMI result; complete mobile, accessibility, household, and purchase/access QA; and confirm through beta evidence that members understand the saved decision and experience less burden. The implementation score is 94/A, but the Guide remains unavailable for sale until these gates pass.

### New Parent

**What works:** It covers insurance selection, Medicaid/CHIP/WIC, leave, childcare, year-one costs, life insurance, guardianship, and household labor. The breadth and emotional fit are excellent.

**Blocking issue:** A Trump Account is a child IRA, not an ABLE account. The IRS describes only a possible qualified rollover to an ABLE account at age 17; the two account types are not interchangeable. The Markdown source and 2026 dependent-care FSA limit were corrected during this audit, but the corrected content is not live until published and verified in Supabase.

**Required upgrade:** Correct the account section immediately. Replace California-first assumptions with a state/location intake, show the exact leave and benefits path available to the household, build a month-by-month birth-year cash calendar, and save childcare and coverage comparisons.

**What prevents overdelivery:** FAFSA parent/contributor rules and deadlines are year-specific. “September of senior year” should not be a standing deadline, and test-score/merit statements need school-specific qualification.

**Upgrade completed July 20, 2026:** The Guide is now versioned by FAFSA award year and produces a saved school-offer comparison, contributor/deadline plan, four-year family/student funding record, cumulative debt view, and parent borrowing boundary. Financial Aid Comparison now accepts current disbursement-year rates, explicitly labels its four-year repeated-offer assumption, removes ratio-based affordability verdicts, and saves the selected school record. Parent Loan Comparison replaces hard-coded rate/fee values and a fabricated retirement target with current entered disclosures, a protected cash floor, member-selected path, and saved outcome. FAFSA Remarriage Planner no longer estimates SAI from obsolete formulas, uses the outdated custodial-parent shortcut, assumes an October 1 opening date, multiplies hypothetical aid differences, or advises around marriage timing; it now saves the official award-year contributor, account, deadline, and special-circumstance workflow. Current FSA, Education, IRS, and College Scorecard sources are embedded.

**Required upgrade:** Save a decision memo comparing guaranteed and risk-adjusted compensation, a coverage-gap plan, runway at multiple dates, and a before-notice/after-exit checklist.

The implementation score is 94/A, but the Guide remains unavailable for sale until these gates pass.

### Divorce

**What works:** Asset/debt inventory, single-income rebuilding, housing, children, and especially QDRO readiness and tracking form a valuable decision bundle.

**Blocking issues:** Immediate financial actions can have legal consequences during separation or under temporary orders. The guide needs explicit instruction not to transfer, hide, close, or materially alter joint assets without appropriate legal advice. It also lacks a safe path for financial abuse and device/account privacy.

**Required upgrade:** Add state-specific legal routing, a safety/privacy intake, temporary-order flags, a settlement scenario comparison, and a professional handoff packet. Keep Krovos in organize/model/refer territory.

### Caregiving

**What works:** It appropriately measures both the parent's care cost and the caregiver's lost wages and retirement trajectory. That integration is a credible Krovos differentiator.

**What prevents overdelivery:** Medicare is only one part of the funding problem. Medicaid eligibility, home- and community-based waivers, VA benefits, state paid-family-leave or paid-caregiver programs, and local care navigation are missing or too light.

**Required upgrade:** Add benefits screening, legal-authority status, a shared sibling/care-team workspace, a written cost-sharing plan, and location-specific care options. Save the caregiver's own retirement and career protection plan.

### Disability

**Naming decision:** **Disability** and `/disability` are founder-approved. “Disability Insurance” was too narrow because the guide concerns income loss, SSDI, medical costs, retirement effects, business continuity, and insurance.

**What works:** The STD/LTD/SSDI offset model and policy-gap analysis can bring advisor-grade reasoning to consumers.

**Blocking issues:** The current guide is still organized primarily around insurance. It lacks a complete diagnosis-to-claim-to-return-to-work flow and no standardized action plan or source section is present.

**Required upgrade:** Add FMLA/ADA and employer-accommodation routing, state disability and paid-leave programs, SSI/SSDI and health-coverage transitions, claim documentation, appeals/professional help, return-to-work scenarios, and a saved month-by-month income/coverage plan.

### Widowhood

**What works:** Separating urgent tasks from decisions that can wait is exactly right for a grieving user. The checklist, survivor benefit, single-income, emergency-fund, and credit tools form a promising bundle.

**Blocking issues:** The guide described the 10-year inherited-account rule as broadly applicable to non-spouse beneficiaries, but official IRS rules distinguish eligible designated beneficiaries. It also attributed the rule to SECURE 2.0 rather than the 2019 SECURE Act and oversimplified Social Security timing. These passages were corrected in the Markdown source during this audit but still require Supabase publication, live verification, and a full expert review of the remaining guide.

**Required upgrade:** Correct and source the retirement and survivor-benefit sections. Turn the first year into a saved, date-aware checklist; add unmarried-partner and dependent-child branches; include fraud/scam protection; and generate an attorney/CPA/SSA handoff list.

### Blended Family

**What works:** It connects remarriage, inheritance, QTIP/bypass decisions, college aid, prior obligations, household structure, and retirement. Those are usually treated separately elsewhere.

**Blocking issue:** The guide said ex-spouse Social Security entitlement was preserved by remarriage after age 60, conflating survivor benefits on a deceased former spouse's record with divorced-spouse retirement benefits on a living former spouse's record. The Markdown passage and a separate “two high-earning years” typo were corrected during this audit; the corrections still require Supabase publication and live verification.

**Required upgrade:** Add a sequenced action plan, Krovos Guide prompts, state-law routing, child-specific inheritance and college scenarios, a household obligations agreement, and a professional handoff packet.

### Estate Planning

**What works:** It explains the functional differences among wills, beneficiary designations, trusts, powers of attorney, guardianship, and blended-family risks in plain language.

**What prevents $49 value:** High-quality explanations and document checklists are widely available free, while document-generation services offer execution. Krovos has not chosen to draft documents, so the premium value must come from organization, modeling, and professional handoff.

**Required upgrade:** Keep the product in organize-and-refer territory. Produce a saved estate readiness inventory, beneficiary discrepancy report, liquidity/tax flags, guardian/decision-maker record, document-location map, attorney intake packet, and recurring life-event review reminders.

### Inheritance

**What works:** The guide correctly slows the user down, distinguishes account and property types, addresses basis and inherited-retirement decisions, and recognizes grief and family pressure.

**What prevents overdelivery:** Executor responsibilities and beneficiary decisions are mixed together. Inherited-account rules depend on beneficiary class, date of death, account type, and the owner's required-beginning-date status.

**Required upgrade:** Branch the guide by role and asset type, create a deadline calendar, store date-of-death valuation and basis documents, and produce a reversible-versus-irreversible decision list with professional routing.

## Portfolio-Wide Launch Blockers

### 1. The purchase promise contradicted tool access — copy resolved, behavioral QA pending

Founder policy is now explicit: a guide is a one-time enhancement saved to the Krovos account, and an active subscription is required to access it. Purchase and gate copy now explains that access returns without another guide purchase after re-subscription. Cancellation, deadline, and re-subscription behavior still require end-to-end QA.

### 2. The Markdown repository is not the live publishing source

The app reads purchased guide content from Supabase `guide_content`, while these Markdown files live in a separate repository. Updating a Markdown guide does not prove the live guide changed. A versioned publishing process and post-publish comparison are required.

### 3. Sixteen guides lack user-facing sources

Gig Work contains a dated official-reference section. The other 16 source files contain no direct source links. High-stakes tax, legal, benefits, healthcare, immigration, housing, education, and retirement claims need visible official sources and a “last verified” date.

### 4. Saved action plans are built; tool-result persistence remains

Every paid guide now receives a deterministic personalized plan with saved action-completion state in the deployed `guide_plans` table. Each guide-specific tool must still be verified to write its meaningful scenario, selected decision, and output summary to the account rather than relying on browser-local state.

### 5. Personalization is not consistently verified

The approval document says the 27 newer guide tools were not individually audited for Life Graph prefill. A gated calculator with generic blank inputs is useful, but it does not fully deliver the Krovos promise of understanding the user's whole situation.

### 6. There is no documented regulatory update system

Many guides contain annual limits, government fees, program rules, deadlines, and state-specific claims. Each such claim needs an owner, official source, review cadence, and expiry behavior. A stale value should degrade to a warning and official link rather than remain presented as current.

### 7. Premium outcomes are standardized in design; live verification remains

Every $49 guide is now designed to produce a durable outcome, not merely access to prose and separate tools. Live QA must confirm this minimum Krovos premium package:

1. A five-minute “what should I do first?” result.
2. A Life Graph-prefilled decision path.
3. A saved 30/60/90-day plan or event-specific timeline.
4. Saved tool scenarios and a plain-language decision summary.
5. Official sources with a last-verified date.
6. Clear professional-help triggers and an exportable handoff packet.
7. Krovos Guide prompts tied to the user's actual result.
8. Reminders when a deadline, assumption, or life event changes the plan.
