# Divorce
*A Krovos Life Phase Guide, $49 one-time purchase*

---

## The outcome

This Guide organizes the financial transition without telling a member what to hide, transfer, close, waive, sign, or settle. It produces a safety-aware inventory, temporary cash plan, settlement scenarios, QDRO handoff, post-decree implementation tracker, and rebuilt single-household plan using data Krovos already has.

## Safety and privacy come first

If there is coercion, stalking, violence, surveillance, or financial abuse, do not use a shared device, email, phone plan, cloud account, mailing address, or password manager to create a secret plan. Contact a qualified domestic-violence advocate or attorney from a safer device and follow their technology-safety guidance. Call 911 for immediate danger.

Krovos does not advise secretly moving or concealing money, closing joint accounts, changing insurance, removing access, selling property, changing beneficiaries, stopping payments, or signing an agreement. Temporary orders, automatic court rules, fiduciary duties, state marital-property law, and safety needs can make an ordinary financial action legally consequential. Confirm permitted actions with a family-law attorney in the relevant state.

## 1. Create the source-backed inventory

For each asset, debt, income source, insurance policy, benefit, tax item, business, trust, digital asset, household obligation, and expected legal cost, save title/borrower, approximate value, balance, basis, beneficiary, document source, last verified date, disputed status, and attorney question.

Pull the member's own credit reports from the official authorized source. Do not attempt to access another person's report or credentials. Distinguish joint borrower, co-signer, authorized user, and account owner. A decree allocating joint debt does not itself release a borrower; the creditor or refinance must do that.

Preserve statements around separation and every valuation date counsel requests. Do not upload intimate communications, children's confidential records, or the other party's credentials to Krovos.

## 2. Stabilize cash without changing the case

Build a temporary-order-aware cash calendar: accessible cash the member is permitted to use, current authorized income, essential expenses, joint bills, legal costs, support paid or received only when ordered or agreed, healthcare, housing, childcare, tax payments, and due dates. Mark every joint or disputed item for attorney review.

Emergency Fund Priority and Single Income Planner use user-selected cash floors and verified post-separation income. They do not assume an individual account should be funded from joint money or that three or six months is universally correct. Save the 30-, 90-, and 180-day runway and the action if cash crosses the floor.

## 3. Compare settlement scenarios, not headline values

For each attorney-approved scenario, compare after-tax and after-debt outcomes, liquidity, basis, sale costs, refinance feasibility, insurance, retirement timing, pension survivor rights, support, childcare, college obligations, business risk, and implementation dates. A retirement asset, home equity award, and equal cash award are not equivalent.

The home scenario requires an actual lender path, title plan, mortgage release or refinance date, maintenance, tax, insurance, buyout funding, sale alternative, and consequence if financing fails. Do not represent loan approval or legal feasibility.

Save the selected negotiation scenario as a working memo with assumptions and unresolved attorney, tax, plan-administrator, lender, and insurance questions. It is not a settlement recommendation.

## 4. Handle retirement through the correct channel

Employer plans, pensions, government/military plans, and IRAs use different processes. DOL guidance confirms an ERISA plan generally needs a valid QDRO before paying an alternate payee despite decree language. Gather plan name, type, summary plan description, model procedures, statements, valuation date, award language, gains/losses and loan treatment, survivor benefit, payment start, and tax/rollover instructions.

QDRO Readiness creates the professional handoff. QDRO Tracker records exact decree/order terms and status; its estimate never overrides the administrator. Do not use a generic pension-growth range or assume a coverture formula unless the order and plan confirm it.

IRA transfers incident to divorce require the decree or qualifying written instrument and custodian process. A withdrawal is not the same as a tax-free transfer. Confirm execution before funds move.

## 5. Account for children, tax, coverage, and implementation

Record temporary and final terms for support, custody-related costs, uninsured healthcare, childcare, activities, education, tax-dependency claims, information access, insurance, and life-insurance security. Krovos does not calculate legal support or custody outcomes.

Tax filing status usually depends on marital status at year-end, but head-of-household, dependent, child-credit, alimony, property-transfer, sale, basis, estimated-tax, and innocent-spouse rules require the actual order and facts. Route to current IRS guidance or a tax professional.

Track every post-order implementation item: deed and title, refinance/release, account transfer, QDRO qualification, insurance, beneficiaries when legally permitted, payroll withholding, support systems, autopay, address, estate documents, digital access, and proof of completion. A signed decree is not proof a third party implemented it.

## The professional handoff packet

Export a concise packet with the inventory, missing documents, cash calendar, settlement scenarios, account/debt responsibility map, QDRO plan list, tax/basis questions, insurance gaps, children's obligations, temporary-order flags, implementation tracker, and the member's questions. Exclude passwords and unnecessary intimate or privileged material.

## The saved 90-day plan

- **Now:** use the safety/privacy path; preserve records; identify orders and deadlines; stabilize permitted cash, housing, healthcare, food, transport, childcare, and counsel access.
- **During the case:** maintain the inventory; compare attorney-approved scenarios; prepare each QDRO; update the solo plan; record every assumption and missing confirmation.
- **After an order:** execute and prove each transfer, release, beneficiary, coverage, tax, estate, and account change; review at 30 and 90 days and after tax filing.
