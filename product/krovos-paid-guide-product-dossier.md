## The relationship between Core and a paid Guide

Core tells the member what their financial life looks like across the Life Graph, Life View, accounts, household, goals, cash flow, debt, insurance, benefits, retirement, and recurring planning tools. A paid Guide must not sell that information back to them.

Each Guide earns its $49 price by applying the member's existing Core context to one demanding life phase and producing a durable outcome:

1. Krovos carries forward facts it already knows without another generated report or additional AI charge.
2. The member confirms changed or missing facts rather than repeating onboarding.
3. A deterministic personalized plan shows what matters now, next, and later.
4. Life-phase tools turn choices into saved scenarios or decision records.
5. The Guide sequences deadlines, documents, responsible people, and professional handoffs.
6. The result feeds the member's ongoing Krovos plan so Core becomes more useful after the life event.

The A-grade score is a product-quality score. It is not permission to sell. Every Guide must also pass the hard release gates in `guide-approval-criteria.md`: current-source review, fail-closed purchase and subscription gates, correct prefill, account persistence, missing-data safety, household privacy, cancellation/re-subscription behavior, mobile/accessibility QA, and successful beta burden-reduction evidence.

## 1. Early Career — 95/A

**What it does:** Converts a first substantial job, first paycheck, student debt, benefits enrollment, housing decision, and mixed W-2/1099 income into one 90-day launch plan.

**How it helps:** New workers commonly make several expensive decisions at once with little context. The Guide orders them, preserves deadlines, and shows how compensation, debt, benefits, housing, cash, and credit affect one another.

**Core layer:** Reuses income, benefits, debts, cash, goals, household, and housing intent. It adds a phase-specific decision record rather than duplicating the Core budget, debt, or savings tools.

**Tools:**

- **First Job Decisions:** records compensation and benefits, employer match and vesting, tax-treatment questions, enrollment decisions, and first-paycheck allocation.
- **Student Loan Repayment Planner:** stores official current repayment options, loan timing, servicer results, and confirmed PSLF facts without inventing changing eligibility or forgiveness estimates.
- **First Apartment Calculator:** combines written move-in costs, monthly margin, and protected post-move cash into a lease-readiness record.

**Why it earned A:** It produces a saved cross-decision plan; removes generic salary, rent, Roth, and student-loan prescriptions; uses official confirmation paths; and ends with first-week, first-paycheck, and day-90 outcomes. The combination is materially more valuable than three free calculators because it connects the decisions to the member's actual financial launch.

It replaces scattered checklists with a coordinated first-year plan.

## 3. Immigration — 94/A

**How it helps:** Members see which facts must come from counsel, USCIS, the IRS, or a financial institution and can protect the household from an income interruption or unexpected process cost.

**Core layer:** Reuses location, household income and spending, cash runway, existing status facts, process costs, goals, and remittance information. It adds a status-aware timeline and professional question set.

**Tools:**

- **Work Authorization Gap Planner:** models essential spending and available cash against a member-confirmed authorization timeline, with pending income excluded.
- **Green Card Cost Planner:** organizes current member-entered government, document, travel, and professional costs by stage rather than hardcoding changing fees.
- **Credit Builder:** creates a provider-confirmed U.S. credit sequence from the member's identification, banking, and credit starting point.

**Why it earned A:** It removes broad legal and banking promises, uses verified inputs, separates tax residency from immigration status, adds FBAR and professional routing, and creates a saved contingency and cost plan. Its value is coordination and risk reduction, not a generic visa explainer.

## Portfolio overdelivery test

Every paid Guide must answer “yes” before sale:

- Does it use Core context visibly and avoid duplicate intake?
- Does it solve a life-phase job Core does not already solve?
- Does it produce a saved plan, decision, timeline, or handoff artifact?
- Do its tools work together rather than behave like unrelated calculators?
- Does it reduce work, confusion, risk, or professional preparation time by more than $49?
- Does it leave the member's ongoing Krovos plan better than before purchase?
- Are uncertain facts labeled and important claims verified from current authoritative sources?
- Are professional boundaries, safety paths, and household privacy appropriate?
- Do access, persistence, cancellation, and re-subscription work exactly as promised?
- Did beta users confirm burden relief and genuine usefulness?

The source and product designs meet this standard. The last four questions require migration, live QA, and beta evidence before the commercial claim is final.
