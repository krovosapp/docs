# Disability

## The outcome

This upgrade creates a saved income-continuity and care-cost plan when illness or injury changes work. Krovos starts with the member's existing household plan, employment, benefits, accounts, cash flow, and goals, then asks only for changed facts. No additional AI report is required.

## Stabilize first

Krovos separates five workstreams: medical safety, job and leave, disability claims, health coverage and care costs, and household cash. The member assigns an owner and due date to every action. If the member cannot act, the plan identifies the person with appropriate legal authority; it does not assume a spouse or family member can manage every account.

## Build the verified benefit timeline

The Disability Benefits Calculator uses member-entered plan documents and agency estimates. It records the definition of disability, covered compensation, elimination period, benefit percentage and cap, duration, exclusions, offsets, tax treatment, claim status, appeal deadline, and return-to-work provisions for each source.

Short-term disability, long-term disability, paid leave, workers' compensation, Social Security Disability Insurance, Supplemental Security Income, state programs, and employer pay are not automatically additive. Krovos presents a scenario only after the member confirms the coordination rule; unconfirmed benefits remain pending.

## Protect work and coverage

The plan prompts the member to obtain written information about leave, accommodations, premiums, job protection, claim procedures, and coverage end dates. Disability benefits and job-protected leave are different questions. Krovos prepares an HR and insurer question list and routes accommodation questions to official resources or counsel without deciding legal eligibility.

Health coverage scenarios use the actual employer plan, spouse or partner plan, COBRA notice, Marketplace option, Medicaid pathway, and Medicare status available to the household. The Medical Cost Planner uses the plan's deductible, out-of-pocket maximum, network, prescriptions, recurring care, travel, equipment, and caregiver costs—not national averages presented as personal costs.

## Create the cash runway

Krovos compares confirmed after-tax income with essential household and care costs by month. It distinguishes approved, pending, denied, and appealed income. The member chooses a protected cash floor and which expenses may be paused. The plan does not prescribe withdrawals, debt actions, or asset sales; it identifies the cost, deadline, tax question, and professional review required.

## Claim and appeal control center

The saved plan stores every claim, contact, document, deadline, decision, and next action. It includes medical evidence requests, attending-provider statements, employer forms, insurer correspondence, agency notices, appeal rights, premium payments, and communication logs. Krovos never predicts approval.

## Return to work or long-term transition

The member can model full return, phased return, accommodation, different work, or continued disability. Each scenario displays household cash flow, benefit changes, coverage effects, and unanswered questions. Business owners receive a continuity branch covering authorized access, payroll, customer obligations, insurance, and succession contacts.

## The 90-day plan

- **Now:** safety, authority, coverage, leave, essential cash, and claim deadlines.
- **30 days:** verified benefit timeline, care-cost view, HR/insurer handoff, and document log.
- **60 days:** pending or appealed claims, coverage alternatives, tax review, and household adjustments.
- **90 days:** return-to-work scenarios or a longer-term income and care plan.

## Sources and review standard

Verify current facts with the member's plan documents and administrators, the Social Security Administration, Department of Labor, EEOC, HealthCare.gov, Medicare, Medicaid, and state agencies. State programs and employer plans vary. Review whenever a claim, diagnosis, job, coverage, or household fact changes.
