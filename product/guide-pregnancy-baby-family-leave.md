## The outcome

This Guide turns the member's existing Krovos plan into a birth, adoption, or foster-placement operating plan: coverage deadlines, leave income, medical and childcare cash flow, dependent protection, and a first-year household system. It reuses known income, spending, benefits, insurance, goals, and family data without another AI report.

## Start with the event and the real deadlines

Record expected or actual birth/placement date, state, each parent's employer and plan, coverage source, adoption/foster path if applicable, and every deadline confirmed from the plan or agency.

Pregnancy does not itself create a federal Marketplace Special Enrollment Period, although it may affect Medicaid or CHIP eligibility and some state Marketplaces differ. Birth does create a federal Marketplace period, generally with 60 days to enroll. Employer group-plan special enrollment commonly uses a different deadline. Do not rely on one universal “30-day” reminder: save the exact employer, Marketplace, Medicaid, or CHIP deadline and the confirmation source.

Use HealthCare.gov's pregnancy and birth guidance, the employer plan administrator, and the state Medicaid/CHIP agency as the source of truth. If care is urgent or coverage is uncertain, contact the provider and program promptly rather than waiting for Krovos.

## 1. Build the coverage decision

Compare actual plan documents, not “HDHP versus PPO” stereotypes. For each plan, enter annual employee premium, family deductible, family out-of-pocket maximum, coinsurance/copays, employer HSA/HRA contribution, network status, prescriptions and specialist needs, and effective date.

Run low, expected, and high-use scenarios. Premium plus out-of-pocket maximum is a useful ceiling comparison, not a forecast, and the PPO does not automatically win in a birth year. Confirm how individual and family deductibles accumulate and whether maternity, newborn, fertility, adoption, or lactation services have special rules.

For Medicaid and CHIP, eligibility and postpartum duration vary by state. A newborn whose parent had Medicaid at birth may receive automatic coverage under program rules, but the state must confirm the record. Save application status, case number, documents, renewal date, and agency contact.

## 2. Create the leave-income calendar

Federal FMLA provides eligible employees of covered employers unpaid, job-protected leave and continuation of group health benefits under applicable terms. It is not paid and does not cover every worker. State leave, employer leave, PTO, and disability benefits have separate rules and may run concurrently rather than stack.

For each parent or caregiver, obtain written confirmation of eligibility, job protection, pay source, weekly amount, waiting period, duration, coordination order, deductions, benefits continuation, return rules, and claim deadlines.

Parental Leave Planner uses only those confirmed amounts. It saves a week-by-week calendar showing employer pay, program pay, PTO, unpaid weeks, health-premium deductions, essential spending, reserve draw, and the date cash crosses the household floor. It does not calculate state benefits from a generic percentage.

## 3. Build the first-year cash plan

Child Costs Planner starts with the household spending already captured, then adds user-entered medical estimates, coverage cost, supplies, feeding, transport, gear, childcare deposits and waitlists, recurring childcare, leave-period help, and one-time adoption or placement expenses. National averages are context only; written local quotes drive the saved plan.

Protect the existing emergency floor and create separate targets for medical out-of-pocket exposure, leave-income gap, childcare start-up, and irregular first-year costs. Confirm current employer dependent-care benefits and tax rules at enrollment; never choose a contribution from an old statutory limit.

Education savings comes after immediate stability. The 529 projection is a user-selected scenario with editable cost, inflation, return, current balance, and contribution assumptions. It must disclose that financial aid, state tax treatment, fees, investment performance, and future education choices can differ.

## 4. Protect the dependent and the caregivers

Life Insurance Need carries forward income, debt, savings, dependents, and existing coverage, then lets the family choose replacement years, childcare/household-labor needs, education, final expenses, and a protected reserve. A generic income multiple is not a recommendation. Save each caregiver's gap and the professional or carrier confirmation step.

Within the deadline set with a state-licensed attorney, review guardian nominations, wills, trusts for minor beneficiaries, financial and healthcare authority, and successor decision-makers. Confirm retirement, insurance, transfer-on-death, and payable-on-death beneficiaries directly with each provider; a will does not automatically control them.

Also save pediatric and caregiver contacts, emergency instructions, childcare authorization, employer return plan, and the first post-birth tax-document list. Krovos organizes these tasks but does not provide medical, legal, insurance, or tax advice.

## 5. Make household labor visible

Use Life Labor before leave ends. Assign full ownership—not “help”—for feeding logistics, nighttime coverage, appointments, childcare communication, supplies, meals, cleaning, finances, and family communication. Save an on-duty/off-duty plan, backup owner, and weekly reset. Rebalance when leave, childcare, feeding, sleep, work, or health changes.

## The saved 90-day plan

### Before birth or placement

- confirm coverage scenarios, networks, leave facts, enrollment and claim dates;
- fund the leave and medical floors; obtain childcare quotes and waitlist dates;
- close dependent-protection and legal-routing tasks;
- assign household work and backup coverage.

### At birth or placement

- notify the correct plan or agency and save confirmation;
- file only the claims and applications that apply;
- update household facts, coverage, beneficiaries, cash flow, and task ownership;
- preserve birth/placement, insurance, medical, and benefits records.

### First 90 days

- reconcile actual bills, benefits, leave pay, and childcare dates against the plan;
- update the first-year budget and protected cash floor;
- confirm return-to-work coverage and Life Labor ownership;
- set 30-day, leave-end, childcare-start, open-enrollment, and annual reviews.
