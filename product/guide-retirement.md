# Retirement

## The outcome

Retirement in Krovos is a Core operating system, not a $49 add-on. It coordinates when work can stop, how healthcare continues, where income comes from, which deadlines cannot be missed, how annual taxes interact with withdrawals, how either partner is protected, and what life after work will actually contain.

Krovos starts from the member's existing Life Graph, household, spending, accounts, benefits, goals, and prior report. It does not require another AI report. Missing facts remain missing rather than silently becoming zero.

## The saved Retirement Readiness Plan

The Hub organizes twelve connected decisions:

1. reliable income floor;
2. pre-Medicare coverage bridge;
3. Medicare enrollment and IRMAA questions;
4. annual withdrawal and tax plan;
5. pension and survivor elections;
6. applicable RMD age and deadlines;
7. down-market spending response;
8. long-term-care response;
9. survivor household resilience;
10. housing and support needs;
11. the first retired year; and
12. the annual retirement review.

Each action has a status and remains saved to the member's account. Tools inform the plan; they are not isolated answers.

## 1. Establish the retirement spending floor

Separate essential spending from flexible goals and one-time projects. Replace current expenses only when the member confirms they change. Include health premiums and out-of-pocket exposure, taxes, housing maintenance, transportation, caregiving, support for others, travel, and irregular replacements.

Create at least three views: essential floor, expected first-year spending, and high-cost year. The plan should show what is covered by reliable income and what the portfolio must supply.

## 2. Inventory every income source and irreversible election

Record Social Security estimates from the member's account, pension choices, annuities, earned income, rental or business income, and portfolio withdrawals. For pensions, record election deadline, lump sum or annuity, survivor percentage, COLA, guarantee, plan solvency protections, and the consequence for the other household member.

Social Security Comparison presents scenarios, not a recommended claiming age. Health, longevity, work, dependent or survivor benefits, taxes, household cash, and the official benefit record all matter. The Social Security Fairness Act repealed WEP and GPO for benefits payable after December 2023; members previously affected should confirm their record and adjustment with Social Security.

## 3. Build the healthcare timeline

For retirement before Medicare, compare actual spouse coverage, retiree coverage, COBRA, Marketplace, and other eligible coverage month by month. Losing job-based coverage can create a Marketplace Special Enrollment Period, but deadlines and subsidy eligibility must be confirmed.

Medicare's Initial Enrollment Period generally spans seven months around age 65. The plan records Part A and B dates, employer-coverage coordination, HSA contribution timing, drug coverage, Medigap or Medicare Advantage comparison, providers, prescriptions, and any late-enrollment or Special Enrollment Period question.

IRMAA is a Medicare premium adjustment, not merely a tax bracket. Record the notice, tax year used, life-changing-event facts, and appeal question. Krovos does not promise that a conversion, gain, or withdrawal avoids IRMAA.

## 4. Coordinate withdrawals and taxes annually

There is no universal taxable-then-traditional-then-Roth answer. The annual plan compares cash needs, ordinary income, gains, basis, Social Security taxation, pensions, conversions, RMDs, charitable giving, Medicare income, state tax, credits, and future flexibility.

Retirement Withdrawal Planner compares simplified annual scenarios. Roth Conversion Calculator models an entered conversion. Neither tool claims to optimize lifetime tax without the missing future assumptions and professional review.

## 5. Confirm access before retirement age

Early retirees need both a coverage bridge and an account-access bridge. Taxable assets, plan terms, Roth basis, the separation-from-service exception, and substantially equal periodic payments have different rules and risks. Krovos records the confirmed source and question; it does not instruct the member to begin an irreversible distribution schedule.

## 6. Track RMDs using the correct applicable age

Under current law, the applicable RMD age may be 73 or 75 depending on date of birth. IRA and employer-plan timing can differ, and plan terms or ownership can matter. The RMD Deadline Tracker uses birth year and account type as a screen, records the first-year double-distribution question, and routes the final amount and deadline to the custodian or tax professional.

## 7. Plan for market sequence risk

Safe Withdrawal Rate and Retirement Scenarios show planning ranges, not guarantees. Before a market decline, the member chooses a cash reserve, flexible-spending categories, rebalancing rule, review threshold, and actions they will not take in panic. The plan distinguishes temporary market response from a permanent lifestyle cut.

## 8. Build the long-term-care and housing response

Record preferences, likely helpers, decision authority, insurance, self-funding resources, home accessibility, local support, and the trigger for professional care planning. Compare staying, modifying, relocating, downsizing, or entering a care community on cost, access, community, transportation, and caregiver effect—not price alone.

## 9. Run the survivor test

For each partner, model which income stops or changes, housing and care costs, filing status, Medicare and insurance, account access, beneficiaries, pensions, Social Security screening, business responsibilities, and who can act. The result is a list of gaps and professional or document updates, not a promise of survivor eligibility.

## 10. Design life after work

Create the first-year monthly spending plan and a realistic week that includes purpose, relationships, movement, learning, care, household labor, and rest. Partners retiring at different times record household expectations and independent routines. The plan recognizes identity and structure as retirement risks alongside money.

## The annual retirement meeting

At least annually—and after death, illness, relocation, a major market event, benefit election, tax-law change, or large expense—reconcile:

- actual versus planned spending;
- income and withdrawal sources;
- realized gains and projected taxes;
- Medicare and other coverage;
- RMDs and charitable distributions;
- beneficiaries, authority, and estate documents;
- long-term-care and housing assumptions;
- portfolio response rules; and
- the next year's cash reserve and life priorities.

## What makes the Core Hub A-level

The Hub no longer treats the FI number as the product. It connects trajectory, spending, income, healthcare, taxes, deadlines, protection, housing, survivor resilience, and life design in one saved plan. Every tool contributes to a household decision and an annual operating rhythm.

## Official sources and review standard

Verify current facts with Social Security, Medicare, HealthCare.gov, IRS retirement and tax guidance, plan administrators, state agencies, and qualified professionals. Current-rule claims require a visible last-reviewed date and an owner. If a value expires, Krovos should show a verification warning rather than continue presenting it as current.
