# Widowhood

## The outcome

This upgrade turns an overwhelming first year into a saved, sequenced plan. Krovos uses the household, account, income, insurance, beneficiary, and goal information already in the member's plan; it asks only for facts that changed after the death. It does not spend another AI credit.

## Start with role, safety, and authority

First identify whether the member is a surviving spouse, unmarried partner, executor, beneficiary, or more than one. Those roles do not carry the same rights. Before any account change, Krovos asks whether there is immediate safety, coercion, fraud, housing, or cash-access risk and routes urgent matters to the appropriate professional.

Do not pay a debt merely because a collector asks, distribute estate property, retitle assets, close joint accounts, or move money until ownership and authority are confirmed. A surviving partner is not automatically personally responsible for every debt. State law, account ownership, community-property rules, and estate administration matter.

## The first 72 hours

Krovos creates a short control list:

- secure people, pets, home, devices, mail, and essential records;
- identify the person authorized to act and the funeral-home or vital-records process for certified copies;
- preserve near-term housing, utilities, insurance, food, medication, and transportation;
- record employer, insurer, pension, attorney, tax, and estate contacts; and
- pause nonessential decisions without creating missed deadlines.

There is no universal correct number of certified copies. Confirm whether each organization accepts an original, certified copy, electronic verification, or a returned copy before ordering.

## The first 30 days

The Widowhood Checklist becomes the member's persistent control center. Each task stores its owner, status, due date, contact, document needed, and next action. It includes employer benefits and final pay, life insurance claims, health-coverage deadlines, Social Security screening, pension elections, recurring bills, account access, fraud monitoring, estate counsel, and tax records.

Krovos builds a 90-day cash view from known income and bills, then lets the member confirm what changed. Pending benefits appear as pending—not spendable cash. The plan protects an amount chosen by the member for near-term needs before presenting debt, investment, gifting, home-sale, or distribution decisions.

## Benefits and account decisions

The Survivor Benefit Calculator compares member-confirmed scenarios; it does not recommend a claiming strategy or assume eligibility. Social Security must confirm the record, eligibility, application process, and payment start. Employer plans and insurers must confirm beneficiary status, deadlines, taxes, payout form, and survivor options.

Retirement accounts are classified before modeling: spouse or nonspouse, IRA or employer plan, traditional or Roth, and whether the deceased had reached the applicable distribution stage. Krovos then prepares questions for the custodian and tax professional. It never treats an inherited-account transfer like an ordinary rollover.

## Decisions that can usually wait

Unless a deadline, court order, safety issue, or cash need says otherwise, park permanent home changes, major gifts, accelerated debt payoff, investment allocation, account consolidation, and lifestyle commitments. The saved decision list records the decision, deadline, missing fact, professional needed, and the member's chosen revisit date.

An unmarried partner receives a separate branch because access to benefits, property, health coverage, and estate authority may differ sharply from a spouse's rights.

## The professional handoff

Krovos produces a concise packet for an estate attorney, CPA or enrolled agent, benefits administrator, Social Security, insurer, and financial professional: household roles, known assets and debts, beneficiary records, open claims, deadlines, questions, documents, and decisions explicitly deferred.

## The saved first-year plan

- **Now:** safety, authority, essential cash, records, and deadlines.
- **30 days:** claims, benefits, coverage, estate administration, and a stable bill plan.
- **90 days:** confirmed income, tax and retirement handoffs, fraud review, and deferred decisions.
- **Six and twelve months:** beneficiary and estate updates, account cleanup, plan refresh, and goals chosen by the member.

This is a Krovos upgrade because it applies the member's existing plan, preserves progress, reduces duplicate data entry, separates facts from assumptions, and turns professional meetings into prepared handoffs.

## Sources and review standard

Verify current rules and individual eligibility with the Social Security Administration, IRS Publication 559 and Publication 590-B, the Department of Labor or plan administrator, the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, and qualified state-specific professionals. Review at least annually and whenever law or program rules change.
