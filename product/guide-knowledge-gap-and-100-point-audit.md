# Guide Knowledge-Gap and 100-Point Audit

**Reviewed:** July 22, 2026  
**Purpose:** Define what separates the current 92–95/A portfolio from a 97–100 exceptional product and ensure each Guide teaches members how to navigate the life phase—not only how to calculate its financial effects.

## Why the current scores stop at 92–95

The Guides now have A-level product architecture, but a score above 95 should require demonstrated excellence, not more prose. The remaining points come from:

1. **Knowledge-gap closure:** members learn the consequential things people commonly discover too late.
2. **Navigation:** the Guide tells them what to do now, next, later, who owns it, and which deadline or document controls it.
3. **Connected tools:** outputs flow into one saved plan and update Core; no orphan calculators.
4. **Local and plan-specific accuracy:** state, employer, insurer, school, court, plan, and institution facts replace generic averages.
5. **Change handling:** reminders and expiry rules reopen the plan when a deadline, law, assumption, or life event changes.
6. **Professional leverage:** an exportable packet reduces paid discovery time and improves the meeting.
7. **Lived usability:** mobile, accessibility, privacy, household consent, and crisis-state design work under real conditions.
8. **Evidence:** beta users complete the workflow, understand the unknowns, avoid a mistake, or report measurable burden relief worth more than $49.

No Guide should receive 97–100 solely because its Markdown is comprehensive.

## The life-phase navigation standard

Every Guide must explicitly provide:

- **What people often do not know:** myths, hidden deadlines, ownership/authority distinctions, and irreversible choices.
- **What applies to me:** deterministic branching from existing Core context plus confirmed new facts.
- **What do I do first:** a five-minute start and urgent-versus-wait separation.
- **How do I finish:** a dated plan with owners, documents, contacts, and completion evidence.
- **When do I need help:** a named professional or institution and a prepared question packet.
- **What changes in Core:** updated facts, decisions, risks, goals, and future reminders.

## Guide-by-guide path to 97–100

### Early Career

Close knowledge gaps around total compensation, vesting, benefit deadlines, paycheck taxes, credit without interest, student-loan servicer versus official program rules, lease liability, and mixed-income taxes. Add document capture before employer access changes, deadline reminders, first-paycheck reconciliation, and beta evidence that members completed elections and housing/debt decisions with fewer errors.

### Newlywed

The Guide must directly answer the “things nobody told us marriage changes” problem used in the Group A SOAP opera: marriage does not automatically merge credit files, fix beneficiaries, grant access to every account, make a will control beneficiary assets, require a name change, make joint accounts universally best, or make filing jointly universally cheaper. It must navigate special enrollment, ownership and title, state marital-property implications, tax filing, support and debt, family gifts, privacy, financial control, household labor, emergency authority, and post-marriage estate updates. To reach 97+, connect each story revelation to a saved couple decision or completed handoff and validate it with newlywed beta couples.

### Immigration

Add status-specific source links and expiry dates, jurisdiction/professional routing, institution requirement capture, tax-residency and foreign-account decision trees, scam recognition, document-access continuity, and beta evidence across more than one immigration pathway. Never imply immigration and tax statuses are the same.

### Home Buying

Add property-specific comparison, inspection/insurance/title/HOA questions, lender disclosure reconciliation, appraisal-gap and repair scenarios, ownership/title routing for couples, maintenance timing, and a post-close Core update. Beta-test whether the Guide changes the maximum offer or preserves needed cash.

### New Parent

Close knowledge gaps around pregnancy versus birth enrollment events, actual plan deadlines, leave versus job protection, payroll timing, dependent enrollment, hospital billing, childcare waitlists, life insurance versus product sales, guardianship/beneficiaries, household labor, and return-to-work scenarios. Add event-triggered reminders and validate with birth, adoption, and placement pathways.

### College Planning

Add school-specific deadline ingestion, award-letter document parsing or structured capture, annual renewal risk, satisfactory academic progress, transfer-credit risk, tax-credit coordination, 529 ownership/beneficiary questions, and year-by-year reforecasting. Prove the Guide improves school comparison and protects parent retirement boundaries.

### Career Transition

Add offer-document capture, equity/bonus/repayment-clawback questions, reference and record continuity, unemployment routing by state, negotiation log, first-paycheck verification, and a post-transition Core update. Validate both voluntary and involuntary exits.

### Starting a Business

Add state/local registration and tax routing, licenses, beneficial-ownership/current compliance checks, banking and bookkeeping evidence, insurance and contract prompts, hiring/contractor classification, customer concentration, shutdown contingencies, and recurring readiness reviews. Validate the Revenue Target against actual monthly business results.

### Gig Work

Its remaining gap is operational productization: save the recurring monthly and quarterly close, import or reconcile platform income where authorized, trigger estimated-tax and coverage reviews, update concentration risk, and prove users maintain the rhythm for multiple quarters.

### Divorce

Add state-specific source routing, secure-device mode, document and temporary-order deadline capture, child and support scenarios, health-coverage transition, tax-year handoff, settlement-to-implementation reconciliation, and trauma-informed beta testing. Safety must override completeness.

### Caregiving

Add local benefits and care-navigation records, care-quality criteria, authority/document status, medication and appointment ownership boundaries, family consent and contribution agreements, reimbursement evidence, caregiver leave/accommodation routing, and recurring burnout/career/retirement reviews.

### Disability

Add claim-document upload/indexing, notice and appeal deadline tracking, plan-definition capture, accommodation and leave handoffs, state program routing, coverage transition, return-to-work earnings/benefit coordination, caregiver impact, and beta testing with temporary, episodic, and long-term conditions.

### Widowhood

Add institution-specific claim tracking, role-based permissions, fraud and collector scripts, minor-child and unmarried-partner branches, returned/certified-document tracking, decision deferral reminders, survivor household reforecasting, and grief-sensitive usability testing.

### Blended Family

Add private-to-shared consent controls, one record per child, court-order and prior-agreement references, college-year changes, home contribution/ownership agreements, gift and inheritance fairness versus equality choices, family communication prompts, and separate-counsel handoffs.

### Estate Planning

Add exportable attorney intake, secure document-location and access testing, execution/witness/notary confirmation, account-by-account beneficiary reconciliation, business succession questions, digital assets, pet/dependent instructions, and automatic life-event review triggers. Krovos must continue to avoid drafting or interpreting legal documents.

### Inheritance

Add executor-versus-beneficiary task ownership, institution-specific deadlines, date-of-death valuation evidence, basis allocation, property carrying costs, inherited-business questions, disclaimer and distribution deadline warnings, family communication records, and post-distribution Core reconciliation.

## Score policy going forward

- **90–95:** A-level design with meaningful personalized outcomes and safe tools.
- **96–97:** live connected workflow, current sources, exports/reminders, and successful end-to-end QA.
- **98–99:** repeated beta evidence across relevant pathways showing measurable burden relief and mistake prevention.
- **100:** reserved for mature evidence that the Guide is best-in-category, maintains accuracy over time, works across edge cases, and consistently produces outcomes worth materially more than $49.

This policy prevents inflated scores. The next portfolio work should prioritize live evidence and connected navigation rather than lengthening every Guide.
