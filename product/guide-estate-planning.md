# Estate Planning

## The outcome

This upgrade does not draft legal documents. It gives the member a complete readiness inventory, beneficiary audit, secure document map, decision record, and attorney handoff packet using information already in Krovos. It asks only for missing or changed facts and requires no new AI report.

## Confirm people and authority

Krovos records the people the member may want to serve as guardian, personal representative or executor, trustee, financial agent, health-care agent, and digital-asset contact. It stores alternates, location, and discussion status—not a legal appointment. State-specific documents and execution rules require qualified counsel or an approved legal service.

## Build the estate inventory

The inventory covers titled property, financial accounts, retirement plans, insurance, businesses, debts, digital assets, personal property, expected inheritances, and dependent or pet needs. Every item records owner, title, beneficiary or transfer mechanism, approximate value, document location, and last verification date.

## Run the beneficiary and ownership audit

The Beneficiary Checker compares the member's stated intention with the recorded title and beneficiary designation. It flags missing, outdated, inconsistent, contingent, minor-child, special-needs, former-partner, and deceased-beneficiary scenarios for confirmation. It does not claim that a will controls every asset or automatically instruct the member to change a designation.

## Decide what counsel needs to solve

Krovos screens for minor children, blended families, unmarried partners, disability or benefits needs, business ownership, real estate in multiple states, foreign property or citizenship, likely conflict, charitable goals, privacy needs, and potential estate or inheritance tax exposure. Each positive answer becomes an attorney question.

The Estate Tax Checker is a screening tool only. It uses a verified current federal threshold and member-confirmed state rules, labels estimates and missing inputs, and never applies a flat rate as an owed-tax calculation. Portability, basis, gifting, trusts, and tax elections go to an estate attorney and tax professional.

The QTIP vs. Bypass Comparison remains an educational handoff tool. It may compare goals and tradeoffs, but it does not select a trust or present a structure as a strong fit.

## Create the secure document map

Krovos stores a map—not secret keys or document contents unless the member intentionally uploads them—showing where the will, trusts, powers of attorney, health directives, deeds, policies, business documents, tax records, and instructions can be found. The plan records who knows how to access them and what should never be placed in an ordinary note.

## The attorney handoff packet

The packet contains household and dependent facts, inventory, beneficiary conflicts, named-role candidates, state and property connections, goals, open questions, existing documents, and decisions the member has not made. After the meeting, the member records counsel's instructions and execution requirements rather than Krovos interpreting documents.

## The 90-day plan

- **Now:** inventory, dependents, current documents, urgent beneficiary conflicts, and access risks.
- **30 days:** decisions and attorney questions; schedule appropriate professional help.
- **60 days:** draft and review through the chosen provider; verify titles and designations separately.
- **90 days:** execute correctly, store the document map, notify responsible people, and assign reviews.

Annual reminders and life-event triggers reopen only affected sections after marriage, divorce, birth, death, relocation, business changes, large asset changes, or a change in intended decision-makers.

## Sources and review standard

Verify current federal tax figures with the IRS, retirement and benefit rules with plan administrators, and all document, probate, guardianship, property, and state-tax questions with qualified state-specific professionals. Review official source dates before publication and at least annually.
