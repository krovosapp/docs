## **What Krovos Is**

Krovos is the first Life Navigation company.

It is a financial tool — and more. A technology platform — and more. A data-driven system — and more. Life Navigation means understanding the whole person: their finances, their family, their career, their home, their health, their goals, and the decisions that connect all of these things across every life phase.

Most of the things that shape our lives are never taught. School covers geometry but not how to file taxes. Trigonometry but not how to evaluate a job offer. The periodic table but not how to choose health insurance. Krovos fills the gap left by every institution that should have prepared us but didn't.

## **Life Phases — The Core Organizing Principle**

Krovos organizes around life phases, not demographics. Where a person is in their life determines what they actually need — not how old they are, how much they earn, or what financial products they own.

Life phases are inferred from what Krovos learns — never self-reported, never shown as a label. The product meets you where you are. Fully. Completely. It adjusts to you, not to a profile. This is what makes Krovos a life navigation system rather than a categorization engine.

## **The Smart Start Flow**

Smart Start is the onboarding experience. It reads your documents first, then confirms what was found, then fills in the gaps — minimizing the burden on the user while maximizing depth of understanding. Ten steps. Built around a simple principle: the burden of being understood belongs to Krovos, not to the person.

| **1** | **Identity** Name and state. Two questions. Everything else comes from documents. |
| --- | --- |

| **2** | **Documents** Upload pay stubs, tax returns, mortgage statements, investment accounts, insurance cards, loan statements, and more. Krovos reads them all via AI extraction. |
| --- | --- |

| **3** | **Confirm** Review what was extracted — paginated by section, with inline editing. Correct anything off. Documents pre-fill the entire review. |
| --- | --- |

| **4** | **Housing Details** Pre-filled from mortgage statement. Add estimated home value for equity calculation. |
| --- | --- |

| **5** | **Income** Income types detected from pay stubs and tax return. Shown as chips — add or remove as needed. Take-home and frequency pre-filled. |
| --- | --- |

| **6** | **Spending** Upload a bank statement for automatic extraction, upload a budget spreadsheet, or enter manually by category. |
| --- | --- |

| **7** | **Investments** Pre-filled from investment statements. Separated by account type and owner — Roth IRA, Traditional IRA, 401k, 457b, HSA, brokerage, 529. |
| --- | --- |

| **8** | **Debt** Pre-populated from pay stub deductions and uploaded loan statements. Add balance and rate to complete the picture. |
| --- | --- |

| **9** | **Retirement Goals** Target retirement age, monthly income needed, lifestyle preference, planning horizon, and top concerns. |
| --- | --- |

| **10** | **Review** Complete Life Graph summary with all data visible before generating the report. |

## **Document Extraction Engine**

Krovos reads documents using the Anthropic Claude API. The extraction engine handles 26+ document types and populates the Life Graph automatically — correctly separating income by partner, separating investment accounts by owner and type, normalizing employer names, and flagging opportunities the user may not know about.

## **The Life Understanding Report**

The Life Understanding Report is the core deliverable. Generated from the complete Life Graph, it is personalized, specific, and actionable — grounded in this person's actual situation, not a template.

| **01** | **Your Situation** A powerful opening that makes the person feel seen. Mentions every household member by name, cites specific financial facts, and frames the household's position honestly. |
| --- | --- |
| **02** | **Financial Snapshot** Combined household income, total invested assets, mortgage details, and an honest assessment of financial health — specific to their numbers. |
| **03** | **What You Are Carrying** A prioritized inventory of burdens — pressures, risks, and gaps — with a specific next step for each. Recognition before recommendation. |
| **04** | **Your Opportunities** Quantified opportunities specific to their situation — employer match optimization, government program eligibility, refinancing windows, insurance gaps, and more. |
| **05** | **What To Do First** Ranked priority actions with timeline, specific next step, and what success looks like. Never generic advice. |
| **06** | **Moving Forward** Strengths, path forward, and one specific action they can take today. |

## **Design Philosophy**

Three principles govern every product decision at Krovos:

| **White-Glove by Default** The burden is on Krovos, not the user. Documents are read. Values are pre-filled. Questions asked are only the ones that cannot be inferred. The product does things for you — not just for you to do. | **Education Woven In** Every unfamiliar term has an info icon. Every recommendation has a reason. Krovos builds understanding through guidance — not alongside it. Users leave more capable every time. | **Specific, Never Generic** Every statement references real data from this person's life. The report knows your mortgage rate, your partner's employer, your youngest child's eligibility for government programs. Nothing is templated. |
