# RevScan Evaluation

**Reviewed:** July 20, 2026  
**Status:** Founder-governed recommendation and commercial-proposal audit complete  
**Inputs:** RevScan Overview, Report, Revenue, 30-Day, and Proposal PDFs plus the RevScan product description

**Operating decision:** The accepted tactics, lead-magnet architecture, lifecycle system, audience-generation plan, and governed profit opportunities are implemented in [Audience Acquisition and Profit System](./audience-acquisition-and-profit-system-2026-07-20.md).

## Executive Judgment

The RevScan correctly identifies four material pre-launch needs:

1. Krovos needs a repeatable organic audience-acquisition system.
2. The lifecycle email program is incomplete and not fully implemented.
3. Beta must establish funnel baselines and collect users' own language.
4. Life Phase Guides need contextual discovery and dedicated explanations once they meet the A-level release standard.

Its score of 29/100 and estimated $30,924 in annual “unrealized” revenue are not decision-grade financial measurements. [RevScan describes itself](https://revscan.ai/) as a diagnostic funnel that routes identified gaps into MarketingSecrets.ai products. The dollar figures are model estimates without disclosed Krovos conversion data, traffic, CAC, retention, or validated LTV. They must not enter Krovos forecasts.

Several recommendations conflict directly with Krovos governance. Krovos is not trying to become a course company, coaching practice, webinar business, engagement-maximizing community, or pressure-optimized funnel. It does not manufacture urgency, prescribe before understanding, target demographic caricatures, or place basic understanding behind extra transactions.

The appropriate response is selective adoption: fix the evidence-backed operating gaps while rejecting the business-model and persuasion tactics that would weaken trust or distract from Life Navigation.

## Governing Filter

Each recommendation was tested against:

- **Understand Before Guiding:** recommendations must follow real user context.
- **Recognition Before Recommendation:** marketing and product guidance must first reflect the person's situation.
- **Minimize the Cost of Being Understood:** do not create new forms, courses, or transactions for information Krovos already has.
- **Reduce Burden:** the interaction must close open loops rather than create homework.
- **Never Profit From Confusion:** pricing, guide eligibility, and offer boundaries must remain explicit.
- **No Manufactured Urgency:** no false scarcity, pressure close, engineered “yes state,” or fear-based conversion.
- **Life Phases, Not Demographics:** acquisition may focus on a life moment, never reduce the audience to one demographic avatar.
- **Peace of Mind Over Engagement:** publishing frequency, community, and retention tactics are subordinate to wellbeing and founder capacity.
- **The User's Interest Governs:** recommendations and add-ons appear when useful, not merely when purchase probability is highest.

## What Krovos Should Improve

### Priority 1: Finish the product promise before amplifying traffic

- Upgrade every paid Guide to A-level and keep all unavailable until the hard gates pass.
- Complete the shared deterministic personalization system, saved tool outcomes, official-source currency, professional routing, and beta completion tests.
- Verify the full Life Gaps to onboarding to Life View journey with beta users.
- Ensure public offer pages accurately explain Core, Guide enhancements, access, cancellation, and re-subscription.

This is the founder-governed answer to RevScan's funnel concern: make the path clear, but do not scale an unverified promise.

### Priority 2: Build the beta measurement system

Create a pre-beta baseline for:

- Life Gaps start and completion;
- waitlist or beta CTA conversion;
- signup and Smart Start completion;
- report generation and report viewing;
- time to first recognition and first useful action;
- Guide interest, eligibility, purchase, start, and completion;
- action and tool completion;
- stated burden before and after;
- questions, confusion, trust concerns, and user-described value.

Traffic and conversion measures are diagnostic. Peace of mind and burden reduction remain the governing outcomes.

### Priority 3: Activate the organic acquisition system

- Use the governed social campaign system across Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, Facebook, and X.
- Choose a sustainable cadence based on founder capacity and content quality.
- Lead with concrete life-phase recognition, not generic feature lists.
- Send interested people to the Life Gaps assessment or current beta invitation, depending on the approved public CTA.
- Begin genuine Dream 100 relationship-building with a small, prioritized list and a documented outreach status.
- Preserve consent, disclosure, attribution, and approval gates for collaborations.

### Priority 4: Finish lifecycle email before adding more funnels

- Reconcile the Group A drafts and implementation.
- Complete the remaining approved sequence copy and actual trigger behavior.
- Resolve sender voice, story classification, cadence promises, unsubscribe, physical address, and Kit-versus-Resend governance.
- Add life-event and behavior-triggered nurture only after transactional correctness and suppression rules are verified.

### Priority 5: Improve contextual product discovery

After each Guide is A-level:

- publish a dedicated Guide explanation page;
- surface it when a relevant Life Graph event or user-stated interest exists;
- explain exactly what Krovos already knows and what the enhancement adds;
- state price and access terms before checkout;
- show the durable personalized outcome, not a stack of inflated feature values;
- measure whether the recommendation reduced burden rather than merely whether it converted.

## Claims Not Accepted as Evidence

The following RevScan outputs are directional marketing assertions, not Krovos planning inputs:

- the 29/100 “AI readiness” score;
- “better than 8% of businesses in your niche”;
- $2,577 monthly or $30,924 annual unrealized revenue;
- consulting values assigned to each lever;
- generalized conversion-lift or average-cart-value percentages;
- claims that a community, course, VSL, high-ticket offer, or named tribe is required for continuity;
- the assumption that missing public evidence means an internal system does not exist.

The scan correctly observed public symptoms in several places, but it did not have authenticated product access, complete repository context, beta behavior, or the Founder Edition. Its recommendations therefore require evidence and governance review before adoption.

## Commercial Proposal Evaluation

**Decision: do not purchase the proposed package.** The useful work can be completed inside the Krovos operating system without adopting the proposal's business model, unsupported projections, urgency tactics, or ownership ambiguity.

The proposal has material credibility and fit problems:

- It identifies “Christine Smith | AI Revenue Consultant” as the presenter and later pitches “why work with Christine” and “I become your launch co-pilot.” That role confusion means the generated document does not reliably distinguish Krovos, its founder, and the proposed service provider.
- It contains unresolved production placeholders: `[Date + 14 days]`, `[Insert your scheduling link here]`, `[Insert Date]`, and `[Insert Christine’s email]`.
- It manufactures a 14-day founding-rate deadline and says the launch window will not remain open. No operational basis for the scarcity is provided, and the tactic conflicts with the Krovos prohibition on manufactured urgency.
- Its monthly pricing is $997, $2,497, or $4,997 for three months, with displayed “founding” prices of $797, $1,997, or $3,997. The document does not establish that buying any tier creates more value than implementing the accepted work internally.
- Its ROI examples depend on unvalidated lead volumes, conversion rates, order-bump rates, and upsell rates. They are scenarios, not Krovos forecasts.
- The proposal reports inconsistent monthly opportunity totals—approximately $888 in one section and approximately $1,295 in another.
- It says subscription revenue costs essentially nothing to deliver at scale. That is false for Krovos, which incurs infrastructure, communications, payment, AI/report, monitoring, compliance, and support costs.
- It recommends immediate paid tests while elsewhere leaning on a “no paid ads” position. Krovos is pre-launch and has no validated CAC, retention, or LTV basis for buying traffic.
- It treats daily video, a named tribe, community, office hours, order bumps, one-time offers, and high-frequency email as a packaged answer rather than testing whether any mechanism reduces burden or serves a real member need.

The proposal is also visually unfinished: it reads as a browser-printed, templated sales document; Markdown-style tables render poorly; page furniture repeats browser output; placeholders remain visible; and Krovos brand or product evidence is not meaningfully integrated.

### Work retained from the proposal

Krovos will retain only the recommendations already supported by its own evidence and governance:

- establish beta funnel and burden-reduction baselines;
- finish lifecycle and trigger-based email;
- maintain a sustainable organic social system;
- develop authentic Dream 100 relationships;
- improve the Life Gaps-to-next-step explanation;
- publish dedicated Guide pages only after each Guide reaches A-level; and
- defer paid acquisition until beta establishes a trustworthy conversion, retention, and value baseline.

No proposal revenue estimate, “pays for itself” claim, founding deadline, or package ROI claim should enter the Krovos forecast or roadmap.
