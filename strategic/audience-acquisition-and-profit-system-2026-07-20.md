# Audience Acquisition and Profit System

**Decision date:** July 20, 2026  
**Status:** Direction approved; launch timing and channel experiments remain evidence-gated

## Executive Decision

Krovos may use more than one lead magnet, but it will not build one for every demographic persona. The portfolio is organized around **entry problems and life moments**, consistent with the Founder Book.

- **Life Gaps remains the universal doorway.** It introduces the Life Navigation category and helps a person recognize what may be missing across their life.
- **Focused recognition tools may be added sequentially.** Each must resolve one high-intent question, produce a useful result without paid AI, and connect naturally to Core.
- **Investment Priority is the first approved focused candidate.** Build the authenticated Core feature first. Near launch, test whether a bounded public version deserves to become a lead magnet.
- **Do not create a library of disconnected freebies.** Every entry point writes to the same consent, source, event, lifecycle, and user-understanding system.

This is a hub-and-spoke model: several truthful entry points, one Krovos relationship.

## Lead-Magnet Architecture

| Entry point | Role | Immediate value | Natural next step | Timing |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Life Gaps | Universal category and recognition entry | Identifies connected life gaps without pretending to know the whole person | Learn how Core builds the complete Life View; beta or waitlist CTA | Keep primary |
| Investment Priority | High-intent decision tool | Organizes where the next investable dollar may belong, with safeguards and missing-fact flags | Core adds the complete household context, spouse coordination, saved plan, monitoring, and updates | Build Core now; evaluate public version near launch |
| One validated life-moment tool | Focused entry chosen from beta/search evidence | Resolves a concrete moment such as job transition, preparing for a home, or preparing for a child | Relevant Core path and, only after purchase/context, an A-level Guide | Do not choose until beta evidence |

Krovos should not launch one magnet for every audience profile. That creates maintenance burden, fragments measurement, duplicates the same user questions, and encourages demographic targeting. A new magnet is justified only when it serves a distinct job to be done that the existing entry points do not serve well.

## Lead-Magnet Release Gate

A focused magnet is eligible only when all are true:

1. The decision occurs often enough in search, social response, beta interviews, or product data to justify a separate entry.
2. The tool provides a complete and honest free outcome; email is not required merely to reveal the result.
3. An optional email action provides real utility, such as saving, sending, or continuing the result.
4. The calculation is deterministic and does not consume the founder's report or Krovos Guide API credits.
5. The difference between the free tool and Core is explicit. Core adds persistent understanding and coordinated guidance, not an artificially hidden answer.
6. Current rules, sources, ownership, privacy, accessibility, analytics, lifecycle suppression, and maintenance are assigned.
7. Life Gaps has a usable baseline, so the new entry point can be judged rather than merely added.

## Investment Priority: Product Before Funnel

### Authenticated Core outcome

Investment Priority should answer: **Given this household's current safety floor, debt obligations, benefits, taxes, goals, and available account capacity, where should the next investable dollar be considered?**

The feature should coordinate:

- employer match and vesting;
- emergency reserves and high-cost debt safeguards;
- HSA eligibility and actual plan facts;
- workplace retirement contributions and remaining limits;
- traditional or Roth IRA eligibility and deduction limits;
- spouse income, plans, and coordinated capacity;
- owner-only or self-employed plan options when relevant;
- after-tax workplace contributions and in-plan conversion only when the plan permits them;
- taxable investing after higher-priority needs; and
- age-based catch-up rules and annual source updates.

It produces a saved ordered list, the reason for each position, the amount or limit still available, missing facts, and the event that should trigger a recalculation. It must not execute trades, claim universal tax optimization, or rank an unavailable account.

### Possible public version

The public version, if beta evidence supports it, should use a deliberately smaller fact set and label its result preliminary. It can identify likely first priorities and missing questions, then offer **Email my priority list** or **Continue with Krovos**. It should never imply the same certainty as the authenticated household result.

## Audience Generation System

### 1. Recognition-led organic content

Every content unit begins with a concrete moment the person can recognize, not a demographic label or generic feature claim. The repeatable structure is:

1. **Moment:** a specific burden, open loop, or decision.
2. **Recognition:** why ordinary advice misses the connected context.
3. **Relief:** one useful way to see or organize the problem.
4. **Next step:** Life Gaps, the relevant focused tool, or the beta invitation.

One strong weekly idea can become a founder post, short video, carousel, newsletter section, and channel-native adaptation. Posting volume is constrained by truth, usefulness, and sustainable founder capacity—not an algorithmic daily quota.

Content pillars:

- connected life decisions that conventional financial tools separate;
- founder and User Zero truth, with privacy boundaries;
- “what changes when” explanations around life moments;
- plain-language corrections to common advice;
- product evidence and burden relieved, once beta users permit it; and
- useful excerpts from approved Guides without giving false personalized advice publicly.

### 2. Lifecycle email by observed signal

Krovos uses one governed lifecycle system with mutually exclusive or intentionally coordinated sequences. Useful triggers include:

- Life Gaps started but not completed;
- Life Gaps completed;
- focused tool completed and result optionally saved;
- waitlist or beta interest;
- account created but Smart Start incomplete;
- Life View available but unseen;
- user-stated life event; and
- A-level Guide relevance after the member has been understood.

Soap Opera structure may be used for truthful story continuity. Stories must be true, permissioned, anonymized, or clearly identified as composites. Every sequence has a purpose, exit condition, suppression rules, unsubscribe path, sender owner, and measurement plan.

### 3. Dream 100 relationships

Build a small, prioritized relationship list by life moment:

- financial educators and newsletters;
- career, parenting, caregiving, home, and founder communities;
- fiduciary and licensed professionals who can review boundaries or accept appropriate referrals;
- employers, benefit communities, and associations; and
- podcasts and creators whose audience already expresses the problem Krovos solves.

The work is research, useful engagement, expert review, co-created education, guest teaching, podcast appearances, newsletter swaps, and transparent referral relationships. It is not mass messaging, scraped outreach, transactional commenting, or undisclosed compensation.

### 4. Search and durable discovery

After A-level approval, each Guide receives a clear public page describing the life moment, the personalized saved outcome, included tools, boundaries, price, Core requirement, cancellation access, and professional handoffs. These pages and focused recognition tools form a durable search surface without turning the product into generic article volume.

## Ethical Profit Architecture

Krovos grows revenue by deepening useful capability after understanding, not by adding pressure layers.

| Layer | Customer value | Revenue role | Governance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Free recognition | Life Gaps and approved focused tools | Qualified audience and trust | Must provide a real result; no hidden toll booth |
| Core subscription | Persistent Life Graph, Life View, plan, tools, and ongoing guidance | Primary recurring revenue | The complete understanding relationship remains the core promise |
| $49 Life Phase Guides | White-glove, saved enhancement for a relevant life moment | Contextual expansion revenue | Sell only at A-level; show why it is relevant and what it adds |
| Professional network | Organize/model/refer when licensed help is needed | Possible future disclosed referral or platform revenue | Quality, conflicts, user choice, disclosure, and legal review required before monetization |
| Employer or affinity access | Life Navigation made available to a defined group | Possible future B2B/B2B2C revenue | Must preserve member privacy and prevent the payer from accessing Life Graph data |

Courses, high-ticket founder coaching, a paid community, inflated bonus stacks, generic checkout bumps, and manufactured scarcity are not part of the current business model.

### Increasing profit without weakening the model

- Improve qualified audience volume through useful recognition tools and partner distribution.
- Improve Core conversion by making the post-result next step concrete and truthful.
- Improve activation and retention by shortening time to first recognition and first relieved burden.
- Improve expansion revenue through contextual A-level Guide discovery, never preselected or urgent.
- Reduce service cost through deterministic personalization and reuse of existing Life Graph facts instead of repeat API calls.
- Measure Guide completion, action completion, burden reduction, and support demand—not purchase rate alone.
- Delay paid acquisition until retention and contribution margin support a defensible CAC ceiling.

## Measurement

Each entry point records:

- source and consent;
- landing view, start, completion, and result view;
- optional save/email action;
- next-step click and account conversion;
- Smart Start and Life View activation;
- qualified Guide relevance and purchase;
- unsubscribe, complaint, and support signals; and
- stated burden or clarity before and after where appropriate.

The system answers three questions in order:

1. Did the person receive useful recognition?
2. Did Krovos earn permission to continue the relationship?
3. Did that relationship produce durable member value and economically sustainable revenue?

## Sequence

### Pre-beta

- Finish every Guide to A-level and keep unapproved Guides unavailable.
- Finish Group A email governance and implementation.
- Define acquisition, activation, Guide, burden-reduction, and suppression events.
- Build the authenticated Investment Priority specification and feature.
- Preserve Life Gaps as the only primary public magnet.

### Beta

- Establish the Life Gaps baseline and listen for high-intent entry problems.
- Test recognition-led content and a sustainable founder cadence.
- Begin a small Dream 100 relationship practice.
- Measure Core activation, retention, burden reduction, and actual Guide interest.

### Near launch

- Decide whether Investment Priority earns a public lead version.
- Choose no more than one additional life-moment magnet from evidence.
- Publish dedicated pages only for A-level Guides.
- Establish contribution margin and a CAC ceiling before any paid traffic test.

## Governed RevScan Takeaways

Adopt: sustainable organic content, ethical Hook–Story–Offer communication, truthful founder narrative, lifecycle triggers, beta baselines, Dream 100 relationships, clear post-Life-Gaps progression, dedicated approved-Guide pages, and contextual Guide discovery.

Modify: use a concrete life moment rather than a demographic avatar; use story sequences without manipulation; use focused recognition tools without email hostage-taking; use partnerships without covert audience extraction.

Reject: daily-volume mandates, immediate paid ads, generic order bumps, forced upsells, artificial value stacks, false deadlines, a low-ticket static report, courses, high-ticket coaching, and community as a retention dependency.

This document governs acquisition experiments unless a later founder-approved decision supersedes it.
