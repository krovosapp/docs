## Category Strategy

Krovos is not entering an existing market. It is creating a new one: Life Navigation.

This distinction is strategic, not semantic. A company that enters an existing category competes on features and price. A company that creates a category defines the terms of competition, builds the first credible product, and becomes synonymous with the category itself.

Nobody owns Life Navigation yet. That is the opportunity.

The conditions that make it possible — AI capable of genuine contextual understanding, data infrastructure for life integration, behavioral science maturity — converged within the last 24 months. This timing advantage is real but not permanent.

---

## Target Market

Every adult navigates consequential life decisions without adequate preparation. This is a universal need — not a demographic segment. Krovos's addressable market is broader than any financial product because it addresses life, not just money.

| Segment | Description |
|---|---|
| **Primary** | Households navigating real complexity — families, mortgages, dual incomes, kids, competing financial decisions. Ages 28–45. Income $50K–$250K. |
| **Secondary** | Single earners in early career phase — first jobs, first apartments, first major financial decisions. Building the foundation. |
| **Expansion** | Pre-retirees navigating transition — Social Security timing, Medicare, legacy planning, caring for aging parents alongside their own retirement. |

**Life phase is the organizing variable — not age, not income.** A 28-year-old expecting their first child is in a different life phase than another 28-year-old who is single. Krovos meets people where they are.

## Retention Strategy

Krovos's retention advantage is the Life Graph. Once a user has uploaded their documents and experienced recognition — the "that's exactly me" moment — switching costs are high. And the product becomes more valuable over time as life events deepen the graph.

**Life Graph Stickiness** — Users who have uploaded documents and generated a report have a high-value data relationship. The graph improves with every life event update. The longer the relationship, the deeper the moat.

**Annual Life Review Cycle** — Tax season is the natural re-engagement moment. New documents, updated Life Graph, updated report, new priorities. Built-in annual renewal hook that is genuinely useful.

**Life Event Triggers** — New baby, job change, home purchase, inheritance — each life event is a natural re-engagement moment and a Life Graph update opportunity. Life moves. Krovos moves with it.

## Pricing Philosophy

Pricing is under active development. The product's value proposition — personalized life guidance that replaces years of accumulated anxiety and thousands of dollars of fragmented advice — supports premium positioning.

**Pricing Principles:**
- Revenue from subscribers only — no ads, no data sales
- Price reflects the value of the relationship, not the feature count
- Annual subscription preferred for retention and cash flow
- Never profit from confusion or manufacture urgency

**Value Reference Points:**
- Traditional financial advisor: $2,000–$10,000/year
- One-time financial plan: $1,000–$5,000
- Estate attorney consultation: $500–$2,000
- Tax preparation professional: $300–$800
- Current comparable apps: $10–$50/month
